Overview

UrMart is the pioneer of lifestyle companies in Southeast Asia, dedicated to protecting our planet, promoting positive nutrition and driving equity. By improving the user experience of both the online marketplace and the ERP platform, we can accommodate each UrMart business. The new design increased the conversion rate by 180% and attracted millions of new customers in six months.

Problem Statement

————————
People don't know that there’re more products that they might need on UrMart. More seasoned users learn this by chance.
Problem for people

From a series of qualitative research, we found that people do not know there’re so many products on UrMart that they might be interested until they see the products. Some people do make an effort to find them, but the majority do not know until we encourage them to try and find more.

Problem for business

The fact that people don’t spend time browsing on UrMart, and they only come when they already know what to buy, keeps UrMart a choice only for a limited group of buyers.

 

 

Goal

————————
Increase the conversion rate by 130% through a better content discovery experience.

The primary goal of the project is to design a brand new e-commerce website in order to accommodate each UrMart business and help more buyers find the items that they might need and purchase more on our platform.

The primary business goals for this website included:

  • Steer buyers towards popular products.
  • Have clear ways of locating specific products.
  • Display the most important information of the products based on buyers' profile.

    • Success definition included:

  • More people engage with the browsing experience (Time spent, Browse rate, and Listing views).
  • More people make purchases (Conversion rate, Number of deals).

    How might we help buyers to discover more products that they might be interested on UrMart?

    ————————

     

    I. Personalized marketplace browsing experience

    Features
  • Get interest signal for cold-start users.
  • Divide users into three categories based on the previous user research.
  • Display content around users' interest when we know; display content that shows the breadth and variety for users.

    •  

    A/B Testing Learnings
  • Results - users start to visit more and spend more time on UrMart finding products (daily time spent per user 5.3%↑, daily visits per user 9.3%↑).
  • Next improvements - despite the successes, there is still room to improve. 1) users want more controls with the content that they dislike; 2) we aren't showing the most valuable content to users at first sight. Our next step is to improve on these problems.
    • II. Simple and intuitive content discovery

    The redesign of listing pages was a project that we spotted the chance where we could insert the accessibility and delightful display in buyer’s browsing experience.

    Features
  • Simple and intuitive listing pages: displaying only the important info for the item - what is it, prices, reviews, nutrition facts, and other qualities.

    •  

    A/B Testing Learnings
  • Results - due to the new listing pages, people browse more items than they used to before (Listing views per user 3-7%↑, Impression 120%↑).

    •  

     

    How might we make the search more discoverable for those who have not gotten the deals they wanted?

    ————————

     

    III. Provide more options whenever available

    Features
  • Category search: since most searched keywords matches category names, to provide the best inventory for the most searched keywords, we enabled category search. So that people always see the best inventory of an intent.
  • Smart saved-search: When a user search something for multiple times in a row, we auto-remind what the user might want to follow up to buy.

    •  

    A/B Testing Learnings
  • Results - Category browse 2-6.8%↑, listing views per user 0.7%-6.3%↑.
  • Results - Increase saved search adoption significantly, around 1.2X - 1.75X.
  • Next improvements - Iterate better save search mechanism based on users activities.

    My Design Process

    How We Discover Problems

    ————————

    We had run more than 10 researches across traditional markets, supermarkets and gyms. Our goal is to find out what could be the opportunities to improve their buying experience and validate our hypothesis. Then we did user segmentation based on the qualitative and quantitative data, depending on their behaviors, major goals and problems in the journey. Before a quarter starts, I held workshop sessions with the cross-functional squad, including sharing sized user insight, scoping for “How might we“, and brainstorming potential solutions.
