Overview

3D Homes Maker turns smartphones into a powerful 3D camera, allowing users to easily create virtual tours of any space. It provides an innovative experience for real estate and architecture industries. Realtors and architects can create realistic and fully immersive virtual experiences by capturing 2D panoramas and converting into 3D spaces.

Virtual tours help real estate agents and homebuyers have an efficient house viewing experience and make it quicker to turn into actual homebuyer buying services. Our PropTech innovations won the 1st Digital Singularity Awards in 2019 for transforming the real estate industry.

Problem Statement

‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ

 

 

 

Homebuyers and realtors spend too much time and efforts on in-person home tours before they could make any decision. Both of them want to have an efficient property tour experience.

 

 

Problems for homebuyers

  • Unrealistic photos: a house might look appealing from photos on the property websites, but in reality, it doesn't work.
  • Lack of a sense of space: even though there are floorplans on the property websites, it's not enough for homebuyers to know the sense of space and environment of a house.
  • Not flexible schedule: most of the homebuyers are busy office workers, they are not flexible enough to schedule time slots with realtors to have in-person home tours.

    • Problems for realtors

  • Time-consuming with low transactions: realtors spend a lot of time and efforts in preparing for an open house, including home tours, staging properties and keeping them in pristine condition for homebuyers. However, the transactions are relatively low.
  • Not applicable existing tools: even though there are other 3D home capture tools such as Matterport, it's mainly designed for professional photographers, which is too complicated for realtors to use.

    The Solution

    ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ‚îÅ

     

    ü§î How might we help realtors create easy to use and high-quality virtual home tours?

     

    As the only product designer in the team, I designed an application that provides real estate agents to capture and create virtual home tours. They can also manage and track their properties with a smooth and efficient process. The solution includes:

     

     

    Step 1. Simple and Intuitive Onboarding Flow

     

    Features

  • Browse and manage all the listings.
  • Create virtual tours by capturing panoramas.
  • Connect fisheye lens with 3D Homes Maker app over Wi-Fi.

    •  

    User Testing Learnings

  • Users tend to give up if they spend too much time on pairing fisheye lens to Wi-Fi, and this interruption stops users from getting into the next step.

     

    Impacts

  • By improving the tutorial of connecting Wi-Fi, the churn rate 28%‚Üì.

    •  

     

     

    Step 2. Straightforward Panoramas Shooting Tutorial

     

    Features

  • Show the animation about how to accurate bearings.
  • Show the tips on shooting in fisheye lens.
  • Show how to set up an appropriate environment for panorama capturing.

    •  

    A/B Testing Hypothesis

  • With the shooting tutorial, users will create more high-quality panoramas than before, and it decreases the number of panoramas that users had to re-capture.

    •  

    Impacts

  • Decreases the number of panoramas that users had to re-capture by 40%.

    •  

     

    Step 3. Capture Panoramas in Just a Single Shot

     

    Features

  • Let users capture complete panoramas by one-click shooting.

    •  

    Next Improvements

  • Add tutorial about how to set up and position the camera in order to create high-quality panoramas.

     

    •  

     

    Step 4. Upload Panoramas in a Virtual Tour

     

    Features

  • Browse all the panoramas
  • Choose panoramas and upload to the cloud.
  • Automatically create virtual tours.

    •  

    A/B Testing Hypothesis

  • Divided panoramas into two categories - Recently Joined or History - in order to decrease the time spent on choosing panoramas.

    •  

    Impacts

  • Decrease the time spent on choosing panoramas before uploading by 35%.

    Identifying the User Pain Points

     

    To understand the home buying process and what problems do they think exist in this process, I reached out to many real estate professionals and potential homebuyers, included:

  • Pretended to be homebuyers and viewed 15+ properties with realtors.
  • Did job shadowing to observe how realtors perform their role.
  • Interviewed 20+ homebuyers and realtors.

    •  

    How could I translate the pain points into design opportunities?

     

    After getting a broad level idea of what the problems were in the home buying process, I conducted user interviews and contextual inquiries to dig deeper into the exact pain points faced by real estate agents and homebuyers. Therefore, I identified the following pain points and design opportunities:

    To make sure that the entire team keeps the target user in mind while designing, I created user personas for both home buyers and real estate agents which we aimed to use throughout the design process.

    Design Guideline

     

     

     

    High-fidelity Idealization

     

    After I had the information architecture, wireframes and design guideline in my hand, I create high-fidelity version of our designs to hand them over to the developers.

     

     

     

    Usability Test

     

    We conducted a task-based usability test with 15 participants and gathered feedback. This was followed by a quantitative survey that asked participants to describe their thought processes while performing the tasks.

    Our general plan consisted of the following steps:

    1. Give a description of the app concept to the users.

    2. Walkthrough users along with benchmark tasks one by one.

    3. Prompt users for feedback after each task about what was smooth or not about the task.

     

     

    I also formed the survey questionnaires to analyze users' feedback by Questionnaire for User Interaction Satisfaction (QUIS) and System Usability Scale (SUS).
