Discover

━━━━━━━━━

 

 

 

To design KKday app, we wanted to understand cognitive processes, goals and behaviors when users use a desktop or a mobile device. We interviewed 30+ KKday users from 18-45 years old. Because half of KKday users came from overseas, we conducted interviews according to users' language ratio and ensured the reference value of the research. (Oversea users: by video interview) The following were feedbacks from our users:

Key Findings from Interviews

━━━━━━━━━

1. When browsing e-commerce app, users are more likely to add products into a wishlist or shopping cart.

2. When browsing e-commerce app, users focus more on photos rather than detailed information.

3. When browsing KKday website, users would like to compare each product by opening many pages and find the best price–performance ratio one.

4. When ordering KKday itineraries, users need to fill in a lot personal information, which is troublesome when using an mobile device. We should provide an efficient and easy ordering process.

5. When traveling, users would like to save all tickets and vouchers in Apple wallet. It can help travelers quickly access their information.

Mockup

━━━━━━━━━

I was responsible for the design of checkout processes, including login, orderlist, filter, voucher and payment pages.

- Login Pages

Users can login by Google accounts, Facebook, or other social channels. For example, we support Naver and Kakao login for Korean, Line and Yahoo login for Japan, Wechat and QQ login for China and HK.

- Order List Pages

Users can check their orders by clicking tab bar. It will show the trips that are coming soon/already had/cancled. Based on the user research, users focus more on order numbers, dates, locations, contact CS and voucher channel.

- Fiiter Pages

Users can filter or sort their products by clicking the button displayed above the tap bar. The products can be sorted by popularity, reviews and price. They can also be filtered by product categories, dates, languages, trip duration and budget. It let users quickly find the amazing trips.

- Voucher Wallet

Since creating a smooth, snag-free travel experience was always our goal, adding vouchers into Apple wallet was important. It can reduce the risk of forgeting paper tickets or even being stolen. Also, users can check information of each itinerary in Apple wallet before heading out. It can release travelers' feeling of nervous and anxiety.

Usability Testing

━━━━━━━━━

 

Before KKday app officially released, we led several usability tests accroding to different features and scenerios. We tested our wireframes against key tasks with InVion prototypes and beta versions. This helped us identify users' frustrations, so we could optimize our design and provided a better travel experience.

We also invited cross-functional teams, such as project owner, front-end developer, back-end developer to observe uability testing. It was helpful to reach an agreement, also came up with a wider variety of ideas.

Followup: How to mearsure the value of our design?

━━━━━━━━━
Heatmaps
Recordings

We analyzed usage data and tested various components in order to understand online purchasing behavior of end users. One way to meausure the design of products was to record heatmaps and videos across all devices every two weeks following with development sprint. Heatmaps aggregated user interactions, including touch, tap, swipe, pinch and scroll in KKday. We also recorded videos for user flow to understand and explain "why" users do instead of "what" users do. It was helpful to know users' real needs behind the bahaviors and kept optimizing our products.
